HIPAA Compliance Statement

Business Associate Agreement (BAA) Framework

Our Role as a Business Associate

Under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), Filrate operates as a Business Associate when providing services to covered entities (dental practices). We execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with every practice partner before accessing, processing, or transmitting any Protected Health Information (PHI).

Safeguards We Maintain

  • Encryption: All PHI is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher and at rest using AES-256.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access limits data exposure to only the engineers and systems required to deliver your specific service tier.
  • Audit Logging: All access to PHI is logged and monitored for anomalous behavior.
  • Data Minimization: We only collect and process the minimum data necessary to perform revenue recovery automation.
  • Secure Infrastructure: Our production environment is hosted on cloud providers with SOC 2 Type II and HIPAA-compliant certifications.

Permitted Uses and Disclosures

We use and disclose PHI solely to:

  • Execute automated patient recall, scheduling, and treatment-plan follow-up workflows on behalf of your practice.
  • Generate aggregated, de-identified operational analytics for your internal review.
  • Comply with legal obligations or public health authorities as required by law.

We do not use PHI for marketing, advertising, or sale to third parties. We do not train machine-learning models on identifiable patient data without explicit written authorization.

Subcontractors

We engage limited subprocessors (e.g., cloud hosting, voice processing) that may handle PHI in the course of service delivery. All subprocessors are vetted for HIPAA compliance and bound by BAAs or equivalent data protection agreements. A current list of subprocessors is available upon request.

Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will notify the covered entity without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 days after discovery of the breach, as required by 45 CFR § 164.410. We will provide all necessary documentation to support the covered entity's notification obligations to affected individuals and the Department of Health and Human Services.

Data Retention and Return

Upon termination of services, we will return or securely destroy all PHI held on behalf of the covered entity, except where retention is required by law or for secure backup rotation schedules. Destruction is performed using NIST 800-88 Rev. 1 compliant methods.
